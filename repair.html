Difference Between Maintenance & Repair

Maintenance service

Preventive tasks to keep the bike running smoothly and avoid future issues. Examples: Goal: To prevent wear and tear, ensure safety, and extend the bike’s lifespan. .

  • 1.Cleaning and lubricating the drivetrain (chain, cassette, and chainrings).
  • 2.Adjusting brake tension and pad alignment.
  • 3.Checking and inflating tires to the recommended pressure.
  • 4.Tightening bolts on the stem, handlebars, and pedals.

Done regularly, even if the bike is functioning perfectly, to prevent wear and tear or malfunctions..

Repair Services

Corrective tasks to fix issues that prevent the bike from functioning properly. Examples: Replacing a snapped chain or frayed brake cables. Fixing a flat tire or replacing a damaged inner tube. Truing a wobbly wheel or replacing broken spokes. Replacing worn-out brake pads or a stretched chain. Adjusting derailleurs to correct shifting issues caused by crashes or wear. Goal: To restore the bike’s functionality after a malfunction or damage.

Standard brake service

A standard brake service ensures your bike's braking system is safe, responsive, and reliable. It involves inspecting, adjusting, cleaning, and sometimes replacing components.

Lifetime brake service

A lifetime brake service typically refers to a service package offered by some bike shops or retailers, where brake maintenance and minor repairs are included for the life of the bike Brake adjustments for proper alignment, cable tension, and lever feel. Cleaning brake components (pads, rims, rotors). Lubrication of pivot points (for rim brakes).

Warranty

Warranties in bike servicing vary based on the service provider, the type of service performed, and the bike's brand and condition. Here's an overview of common warranties and guarantees you might encounter in bike servicing:

  • Coverage: Guarantees the quality of the work done during the servicing, such as tune-ups, brake adjustments, or gear calibrations.
  • Typically ranges from 7 days to 30 days, depending on the shop. If issues arise due to improper servicing within this period, they will fix it free of charge.
  • Applicable to replacement parts installed during servicing, such as chains, brakes, or tires.
  • Varies by manufacturer; some parts have a 6-month to 1-year warranty against defects.

Suspension

Suspension in bike servicing refers to the maintenance, repair, and adjustment of a bike's suspension system, which is responsible for absorbing shocks and providing a smoother ride. The type and complexity of suspension servicing depend on the bike's design and the suspension system it uses.
